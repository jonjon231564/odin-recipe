Cheese Burger

The cheeseburger is an iconic American dish that can be found in diners and restaurants across the country. With its juicy beef patty, melted cheese, and fresh toppings, it's a beloved comfort food for many. This recipe captures the essence of a classic cheeseburger, combining seasoned ground beef with the perfect selection of condiments and vegetables. Whether cooked on the grill or stovetop, the cheeseburger can be customized with your favorite ingredients to make it uniquely yours.

Begin by seasoning the beef patty with salt and pepper, then cook to your desired level of doneness on a grill or stovetop skillet. As the meat nears completion, place the cheese on top to allow it to melt slightly. While the patty cooks, prepare the bun by spreading mayonnaise, ketchup, and mustard on the inside, then layering with lettuce, tomato, and pickles. Place the cooked patty with melted cheese on the bottom half of the bun, then close with the top half. Serve your cheeseburger with a side of fries, onion rings, or a refreshing salad for the perfect meal.

  • 1 ground beef patty (about 4-6 oz)
  • 1 hamburger bun, split
  • 1 slice of cheese (such as American, cheddar, or Swiss)
  • 1 slice of tomato
  • 1 lettuce leaf
  • 2 slices of pickles
  • 1 tablespoon of mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon of ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon of mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil (for grilling)
  1. Preheat a grill or skillet with cooking oil over medium-high heat. Season the beef patty with salt and pepper and cook to your preferred doneness (about 4-5 minutes per side for medium). Place the cheese on top of the patty during the last minute of cooking to melt.
  2. While the patty cooks, spread mayonnaise, ketchup, and mustard on the inside of the bun. Add lettuce, tomato, and pickles to the bottom half.
  3. Place the cooked patty with melted cheese on the prepared bottom half of the bun. Close with the top half of the bun.
  4. Serve immediately with your chosen sides.
